The weekend before Fat Tuesday is the prime time to visit New Orleans for Mardi Gras. This is the weekend when Bacchus and Endymion, two of the biggest parades of the season, roll down the streets of New Orleans. The streets are packed all day Saturday and Sunday with parade watchers.

Since Mardi Gras is a religious holiday, the day itself cannot be canceled.However, last year, the City of New Orleans decided not to have its popular Mardi Gras parades. For 2022, the official Mardi Gras parade schedule has been released and it seems, as of right now, all events will continue to take place.

Although founded in the 1760s, the St. Louis Mardi Gras festivities only date to the 1980s. The city's celebration begins with "12th night," held on Epiphany, and ends on Fat Tuesday The season is peppered with various parades celebrating the city's rich French Catholic heritage.
